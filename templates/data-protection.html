Data Protection

Your data security is our highest priority

Last Updated: 1 January 2024 GDPR Compliant ISO 27001 Certified

1. Our Data Protection Commitment

AtlasNexus is committed to protecting and respecting your privacy and personal data. We implement industry-leading security measures and comply with all applicable data protection laws, including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the UK Data Protection Act 2018.

2. Data Protection Principles

We adhere to the following fundamental principles in all our data processing activities:

  • Lawfulness, Fairness and Transparency: Processing data legally and transparently
  • Purpose Limitation: Collecting data only for specified, explicit and legitimate purposes
  • Data Minimisation: Processing only data that is necessary and relevant
  • Accuracy: Ensuring data is accurate and kept up to date
  • Storage Limitation: Retaining data only as long as necessary
  • Integrity and Confidentiality: Ensuring appropriate security of personal data
  • Accountability: Taking responsibility for compliance and demonstrating it

3. Technical Security Measures

Encryption Standards

  • 256-bit AES encryption for data at rest
  • TLS 1.3 encryption for data in transit
  • End-to-end encryption for sensitive communications
  • Hardware Security Module (HSM) for key management
  • Encrypted backups with separate key storage

Access Controls

  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) required for all users
  • Role-based access control (RBAC)
  • Principle of least privilege
  • Regular access reviews and audits
  • Automated de-provisioning for terminated accounts

Network Security

  • Next-generation firewalls
  • Intrusion detection and prevention systems (IDS/IPS)
  • Network segmentation and isolation
  • DDoS protection
  • Regular penetration testing

4. Data Classification

We classify data into categories to ensure appropriate protection levels:

Classification Description Protection Level
Public Information intended for public disclosure Standard
Internal Business information for internal use Enhanced
Confidential Sensitive business and client information High
Restricted Highly sensitive data requiring maximum protection Maximum

5. Data Processing Activities

We maintain comprehensive records of all data processing activities:

  • Purpose and legal basis for processing
  • Categories of data subjects and personal data
  • Recipients of personal data
  • International data transfers
  • Retention periods
  • Technical and organisational security measures

6. Data Subject Rights

We fully support your rights under data protection law:

  • Right to Access: Obtain confirmation and copies of your personal data
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your personal data
  • Right to Restriction: Limit processing of your data
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured format
  • Right to Object: Object to certain types of processing
  • Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making: Not be subject to solely automated decisions

7. Data Breach Response

Our incident response plan ensures rapid and effective breach management:

  1. Detection: 24/7 monitoring and alerting systems
  2. Assessment: Immediate evaluation of breach severity
  3. Containment: Isolation and mitigation measures
  4. Notification: ICO notification within 72 hours if required
  5. Communication: Affected individuals notified without undue delay
  6. Review: Post-incident analysis and improvement

8. International Data Transfers

When transferring data internationally, we ensure adequate protection through:

  • Adequacy decisions by relevant authorities
  • Standard contractual clauses (SCCs)
  • Binding corporate rules (BCRs) where applicable
  • Specific derogations for particular situations
  • Explicit consent where appropriate

9. Third-Party Data Processing

We carefully select and monitor all third-party processors:

  • Due diligence assessments
  • Data processing agreements
  • Regular audits and reviews
  • Contractual security obligations
  • Liability and indemnification clauses

10. Privacy by Design

Data protection is embedded in everything we do:

  • Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs)
  • Privacy considerations in system design
  • Default privacy settings
  • Minimisation of data collection
  • Pseudonymisation and anonymisation techniques

11. Employee Training

All staff receive comprehensive data protection training:

  • Initial data protection awareness training
  • Annual refresher courses
  • Role-specific training for high-risk functions
  • Regular security awareness updates
  • Phishing simulation exercises

12. Compliance and Audit

Regular assessments ensure ongoing compliance:

  • Internal compliance audits
  • External third-party assessments
  • Regulatory inspections
  • Continuous monitoring and improvement
  • Board-level oversight and reporting
